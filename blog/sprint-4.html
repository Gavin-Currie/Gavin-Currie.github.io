Neuroplasticity

I did a bit of a google search, and came across this article written in:Frontiers.org and how it discusses the brains ability to modify, change and adapt both structure and function
throughout life and in response to experience. Just as individual differences contribute to variability observed in brain structure and function mechanisms of neuroplasticity also show significant variablity across individuals.

I'm doing a bit of paraphrasing here, but the article also discusses the structure of the neuroplastic brain in adults and recovering stroke victims.
With recovering stroke victims, they found it had the ability to dynamically re-route neural pathways to compensate for the lost pathways due to injury or damage, until the original pathways are restored to pre-damage conditon.
Another thing I'm coming across, is also the neuroplastic brain not only dynamically re-routes, it can also dynamically increase cognitive capacity on the fly.
Where some cortical sensory areas may be inhibited, other areas that manage other sensory functions are enhanced, (as evidence is found in optically challenged people, where their hearing is up to 50% more sensitve than sighted people.)
With adults who are learning a new skill or hobby, those pathways that were previously entropied or hardened due to inactivity, experience a "massaging" process at the electro-cellular level.

Growth Minset

Again, I did a bit of research on the topic of growth minset and what that might look like. I came across a website with their list of how to develop a growth mindset.
(I've also added my own embelishments to this list, to en-enforce this to myself.)

  1. Listen to the mindset voice inside of you:There's a battle for your mind going on. Do you want the good to win?
  2. Recognise that you have a choice: Free will and Wisdom from above, will decide destinies.
  3. Talk back with a growth mindset voice: Your mind is a checkout conveyor, replace the negative by pushing in a postitive....beep.
  4. Practice: As you keep practicing this, you will get better at it. Think of it as developing the minds muscle memory.
  5. Find outside help:Finding a good mentor gets half the battle won, right there.
  6. Stop seeking approval of others: You'll just become their slave...
  7. Replace the word "failing" with "learning" Failing is an opportunity to re-route and improve your strategies as part of your success continuity plan.
  8. Take growth mindset action: Now is good...Today is too.

Code readability

This is something that I kind of knew about for a while, but it's good to have a refresher about the topic.
This discusses how your code can actually be cleaner and pleasant to read (from another coder's perspective) through the practice of:

  • Having it all nice and indented. Indentation lines are your freind.
  • Suitable and meaningful names for your variables and functions
  • Consistent naming conventions
  • the strategic placement of comments. (pseudocode)
  • No syntax or logic errors.
  • Good logic flow
