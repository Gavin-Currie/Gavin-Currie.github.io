Emotional Intelligence


hmmm... Emotional Intelligence - what is it?
How does this relate to EQ (Emotional Quotent) and IQ (Intelligence Quotent)?
I did a bit of research, and according to healthline.com :

IQ refers to your intellectual ability. Some of the common elements include:
  • Use logic to solve problems.
  • Plan and strategise.
  • Understand abstract ideas.
  • Learn and adapt to change.
  • Grasp and use language.

EQ refers to your ability to sence emotion in yourself and others.(Sometimes interchanged with the phrase: Emotional intelligence)
If you have high EQ, you may find it easy to:
  • Identify emotions in yourself and others.
  • Empathise with other people.
  • Adapt your feelings and behaviours to different situations.
  • Control your impulses.
  • Withstand temptation and delay gratification.
  • Resolve conflicts with others.
  • Communicate effectively.

Testing:
IQ tests typically measure two different intelligence abilities known as:
  • Crystalised intelligence: which is based on your verbal ability and knowledge and usually improves as you get older.
  • Fluid Intelligence: This gives you the ability to think abstractly and solve problems without pre-existing knowledge

EQ tests on the other hand, rank your abilities in five areas:
  • Self awareness
  • Self regulation
  • Motivation
  • Empathy
  • Social skills


Practice of Emtional Intelligence
Some ways to practice Emotional Intelligence:
  1. The practice of mindfulness: focussing on enhancing a sence of 'engaged: now' and compassion for ourselves and others, to be less reactive, and think about pre-emptive wisdom. Your own style of calm.
  2. Self knowledge and self mastery: This includes being aware of our emotional buttons that get pushed in certain situations, and how to mitigate those reactions.
  3. Create useful mental habits: To be on guard against those negative self-thoughts that creep into your mind from time to time.

Creating good and useful habits takes discipline.
Discipline shapes and improves your character.
Your character shapes your lifestyle.
Your lifestyle shapes your future.

So keep practising these habits of emotional intelligence. The changes will be gradual, but you'll get there. Your goals will be acheived.
